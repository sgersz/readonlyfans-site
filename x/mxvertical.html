Why the Logitech MX Vertical Is My Favorite Mouse (And Why It Beats Gaming Mice for Everyday Use)

I never thought I’d ditch my trusty Logitech ultralight gaming mouse — until I tried the Logitech MX Vertical. After a few weeks of daily use, I can confidently say this is the most comfortable mouse I’ve ever owned.

Here’s why the MX Vertical stands out — and why it might be worth a look even if you’re coming from the gaming world.

Comfort First: It’s Not Even Close

The MX Vertical is built for one thing above all: ergonomics. The vertical design puts your wrist in a natural handshake position, reducing strain during long hours at the desk. Compared to my ultralight gaming mouse, which was fast and nimble but flat, the MX Vertical feels like a relief to the hand.

After a few days, I noticed less wrist tension — something no gaming mouse I’ve used has ever delivered.

Precision Where It Matters

Gaming mice are tuned for speed and ultra-high DPI, but for productivity, the MX Vertical hits the sweet spot. It’s got adjustable DPI (up to 4000), and the precision is plenty for creative work, spreadsheets, and even light gaming. It won’t win you a CS:GO tournament, but for everything else? It’s more than capable. I will add swapping the feet or adding the universal teflon feet fully unlock this mouse!

Build Quality and Battery Life

Logitech nails the build. The MX Vertical feels solid, premium, and well-balanced in hand. The battery life is another win: up to four months on a full charge. That beats most wireless gaming mice, which often need charging every few days under heavy use.

The Only Negative: The Feet

If I had to nitpick, it’s the mouse feet. Unlike some high-end gaming mice that come with smooth Teflon feet, the stock feet on the MX Vertical are… fine. Not great. But here’s the good news: they’re easily replaceable with aftermarket Teflon feet, which turn the glide into pure butter.

Final Verdict

If you spend long hours at a desk, the Logitech MX Vertical is a game changer. It’s the most comfortable mouse I’ve used, it delivers solid precision, and it’s built to last. Gamers might miss the ultralight flicks and RGB, but for everything else — productivity, creative work, or just browsing — the MX Vertical absolutely rules.

TL;DR:

If you value comfort and long-term wrist health, the MX Vertical is worth every penny.
